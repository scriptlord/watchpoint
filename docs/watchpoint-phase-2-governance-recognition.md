# WatchPoint Phase 2 Ideas

Governance, performance review, recognition, and community rewards.

---

## 1. The Core Idea

WatchPoint should start as a safety and maintenance tool.

But once the system is used for real incidents, it creates something valuable:

**a trusted record of how well the community responds.**

That record can later help residents review:

- estate security performance
- responder performance
- facility manager performance
- landlord or estate association accountability
- service provider quality
- repeated maintenance failures

This should be Phase 2, not MVP.

The MVP should first prove that residents can report incidents, responders can act, and the community can see what happened.

---

## 2. Why This Matters

Many estate problems do not continue because nobody complained.

They continue because there is no clean record.

Examples:

- residents complain about broken streetlights for weeks
- security responds late to incidents
- a facility manager keeps saying "we are working on it"
- service providers collect money but do poor work
- landlords ignore repeated safety complaints
- residents argue in WhatsApp groups without evidence

WatchPoint can change this because it records:

- when a report was created
- who was assigned
- when it was acknowledged
- when it was resolved
- whether residents disputed the resolution
- how long the issue stayed open
- whether the same problem keeps repeating

That makes performance measurable.

---

## 3. Performance Reports

Phase 2 can include monthly community reports.

Examples:

- average emergency acknowledgement time
- average maintenance resolution time
- number of unresolved maintenance tickets
- number of disputed resolutions
- most repeated issue category
- responder activity
- service provider response quality
- security performance over time

This gives residents facts, not rumours.

Instead of saying:

"Security is slow."

The community can say:

"In May, the average incident acknowledgement time was 18 minutes. Our target is 5 minutes."

---

## 4. Resident Recognition

Residents should be able to appreciate responders after good work.

This can look like:

- stars
- kudos
- thank-you notes
- helpfulness score
- monthly recognition badges

Example:

After a responder resolves a fire alarm, residents can tap:

**Appreciate responder**

That feedback is attached to the incident and counted in the responder's performance record.

---

## 5. Rewards, But Carefully

WatchPoint should avoid direct pay-per-emergency.

Direct payments can create bad incentives:

- responders may favour residents who tip
- residents may feel pressured to pay after help
- people may game the system
- emergency response may become unfair

The safer model is:

1. Residents give appreciation or feedback.
2. WatchPoint records recognition points.
3. The community reviews performance monthly.
4. The estate association decides rewards or bonuses.

So the money decision belongs to the community, not to one emergency interaction.

Good framing:

**resident recognition -> performance score -> community-approved reward**

Bad framing:

**resident pays responder directly per emergency**

---

## 6. Governance And Accountability

WatchPoint can also help communities make better governance decisions.

Examples:

- replace a poor security vendor
- question estate dues when maintenance is not being handled
- review landlord responsiveness
- escalate repeated building safety issues
- compare service providers before renewing contracts
- decide whether an estate manager is performing

This should not be framed as "the app votes people out" in the MVP.

That may scare estate managers and slow adoption.

Better framing:

**WatchPoint gives the community evidence to make better decisions.**

The community can then use that evidence in meetings, elections, vendor reviews, or formal complaints.

---

## 7. Possible Phase 2 Tables

These tables are not needed for MVP, but they may be added later.

| Table | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `responder_feedback` | Stores resident ratings or comments after an incident. |
| `recognition_points` | Tracks points or stars earned by responders. |
| `performance_reports` | Stores monthly estate/community performance summaries. |
| `community_rewards` | Records rewards approved by the community or estate association. |
| `service_provider_reviews` | Stores ratings and reviews for plumbers, electricians, towing services, and other providers. |
| `governance_actions` | Records formal community actions such as vendor replacement, escalation, or management review. |

---

## 8. What Should Stay Out Of MVP

Do not build these in the first version:

- money transfers
- direct tipping
- elections
- landlord blacklists
- public shaming dashboards
- automated punishment

These features are sensitive.

They need trust, policy, and community buy-in.

MVP should stay focused on:

- incident reporting
- resident confirmation
- responder status updates
- resolution disputes
- maintenance tracking
- audit trail

---

## 9. Simple Pitch

WatchPoint starts by helping communities respond to incidents faster.

Over time, it becomes a trusted record of community performance.

Residents can see what was reported, who responded, how long it took, whether the resolution was disputed, and which service providers performed well.

That record can help estates reward strong responders, replace weak vendors, and hold managers accountable with evidence instead of WhatsApp arguments.
